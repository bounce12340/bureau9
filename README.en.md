# Bureau 9 (行政院第九局) — The Bureau 9 Universe

> A Taiwanese spy-comedy serial ｜ Absurd methods × serious purpose
>
> [中文版 → README.md](README.md)

A unit that appears on no government org chart — the Executive Yuan's Bureau 9. Its safehouses are a shoe store, a renovation company, a tea shop. Its operatives are borough chiefs, temple festivals, and electro-dance Third Prince troupes. Its weapon is whatever truly burns in the hearts of the people of this island.

This repository collects all completed works of the Bureau 9 Universe, organized by series. Everything is written in Threads serial format (8 posts per episode, ≤500 Chinese characters per post).

## Major Series

### The Weaver Tetralogy (antagonist main story — complete)
Told from the perspective of "the Weaver" (Lu Chen), a veteran MSS case officer running the Taiwan line — the mirror image of the Bureau 9 main line.

- **Part I, *Reclamation*** (8 eps / 66 posts) — a case-closing dawn, and a photograph turned face-down
- **Part II, *Shao-kang*** (10 eps / 81 posts) — 1988–1999: where a deep needle comes from
- **Part III, *Weaving the Net*** (8 eps / 64 posts) — four defeats, and a face that stays forever blank
- **Part IV, *The Blade and the Needle*** (10 eps / 80 posts) — a blade and a needle, broken one after the other by the same white hand; final frame: *One Table Across the Sea*

### Spring Soil (side story — complete)
Hsieh Ming-yuan's story: a man who has spent his life weaving nets discovers that a free society is the one net he can never draw closed. 17 episodes / 136 posts in three acts (Weaving / Lights-Out / The Harvest That Cannot Be Reaped), serialized interleaved with *The Blade and the Needle*.

### Reading / Posting Order
*Spring Soil* and *The Blade and the Needle* interleave in six blocks. See `07_設定集與準則/發文排程總表.md` for the global order.

## Craft Rules (excerpt)

- 8 posts per episode, ≤500 characters each; zero em-dashes in prose; coincidence adverbs ("suddenly", "just happened to") are banned
- Show, don't tell; character actions follow motive logic; coincidence never drives key plot turns
- Taiwanese authenticity: Taiwanese-dialect dialogue, local institutions (borough chiefs, temple processions), locally calibrated usage
- Iron rule: the Weaver and the White Hand never meet

Full guides live in `07_設定集與準則/`.
